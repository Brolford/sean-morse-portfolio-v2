## PROJECT 2: Leisure Project
- Slug: `/leisureproject`
- Thumbnail filename: Leisure_CaseStudy_OC_Can.jpg
- Client: Leisure Project
- Agency: Freelance
- Services: Strategy, Brand Identity, Packaging Design
- Sean's Role: Brand - Creative Director

### Page Title
Leisure Project

### Subtitle
Revolutionizing Creator Hydration -- Packaging Design

### Tagline
"A New Era Of Holistic Refreshment"

### Intro Copy
Step into an oasis of Holistic Refreshment -- a lush space where hydration goes beyond revitalizing the body to uplift the mind and awaken creativity. Crafted with hydrating nutrients alongside adaptogens and nootropics to help you take care of both your mind and body. Leisure reveals a new path for future pursuits and past passions. So dive in, have a sip, and take up what inspires you. Whatever drives that inner spark, embrace it and let it run free.

### Problem
Alex & Steve Michaelsen, two brothers and former college athletes noticed a gap within the overall hydration category. With most hydration brands focusing on the athletic space, they wanted to launch a brand that would go beyond this and serve the modern-day creators. Utilizing adaptogens, minerals & nootropics Leisure's unique hydrating blend would aim to provide a sense of holistic refreshment that would inspire creators to be at their mental best.

### Solution
From the ground up the brand was developed to provoke curiosity & exude a calming sensation. The logotype and principal typography promote a relaxing sensation without resorting to "blanding". Through the utilization of a portal, a refreshingly lush oasis of illustrations is revealed that fuses flavor, function, & fantasy. The space invites the discovery of a new refreshing era. The characters were developed to help imbue the brand with the lifestyle component as well as act as personifications of each flavor. Furthermore, knowing the brand was seeking to create a series of NFTs, the hero characters would act as a launch pad for the development of the Web3 assets.

### Results
Upon launch, Leisure was met with tremendous success. The NFTs and associated Hydration Club provided the brand with an immediate community of interested consumers. They also gained media attention from websites like the Dieline, ThingTesting, BevNet, CPGD, and more. Leisure quickly expanded beyond D2C and is in the top five beverages, flying off shelves in Erewhon, Bristol Farms, Gelsons, and dozens of top retail chains throughout California. At the close of year 1 in business, the brand is on a $1M run rate and signed the largest DSD beverage house in SoCal, Los Angeles Distributing, with an equity investment to truly partner up and scale the brand.

## PROJECT 3: Alec's Ice Cream
- Slug: `/alecs-ice-cream`
- Thumbnail filename: Alecs_CaseStudy_Layout_V3-14.jpg
- Client: Alec's Ice Cream
- Agency: Hatch SF
- Services: Strategy, Brand Identity, Photography
- Sean's Role: Design Director

### Tagline
"Happy Gut. Happy Planet."

### Intro Copy
What would happen if ice cream loved the planet as much as we do? Enter Alec's organic pasture-raised ice cream. Slow-whipped and ultra creamy using regeneratively farmed A2 dairy -- it's better for your stomach and the planet. Regenerative farming removes CO2 from the atmosphere for brighter, tastier tomorrows. So for the love of the planet, eat up.

### Problem
Set out to become a leader in super-premium ice cream by redefining expectations on taste & texture, integrity of ingredients, and how ice cream should leave you feeling. Complex story -- where and how to communicate and educate consumers about benefits, mission, and brand vision? With tons of other brands offering craft quality ice cream, how do we stand out on the shelf amongst the considerable competition behind those icy glass doors?

### Solution
By elevating Alec's communication with photography (taste & texture), color, and a compelling narrative, the brand was better positioned to deliver on why they are indeed better tasting and better for the planet. Creating an imaginative world that portrays the planet and cow as companions piques curiosity and invites one to learn more about the product's regenerative qualities. The playful illustrations show tone while highlighting the importance and benefits of regenerative farming practices. The layering of artfully stylized food photography creates a rich sense of indulgence to pay homage to the ice cream's premium quality and ingredients. With solid brand blocking and a newly crafted logo, the prominent hierarchy on the pack with flavor and color cues make Alec's easily stand out while offering an emotional connection to the target audience.

### Results
Launched new flavor @ Expo West 2022 -- NEXTY Award finalists for Best New Organic Food & Best New Dessert. Within the first year, increased distribution by seven times. Featured on Today Show, Bon Appetit, WSJ, NOSH, Huffington Post, E-Online, and FoodBev Media. As of 2022 Alec's became available nationally in Whole Foods Market, their top aspiration when kicking off the project.

## PROJECT 4: V8 / Campbell's
- Slug: `/v8campbells`
- Thumbnail filename: V8_CaseStudy_2023_Logo_Ingredients_Thumbnail.jpg
- Client: Campbell's V8 Juice
- Agency: Hatch SF
- Services: Strategy, Brand Identity, Photography
- Sean's Role: Design Director

### Page Title
V8

### Subtitle
V8 Brand Transformation: Modernizing a Heritage Wellness Brand

### Tagline
"Reigniting The Power of Plants"

### Intro Copy
Wellness is more than an intake of vitamins and nutrients; it's about nourishment that powers your day and leaves you feeling good inside and out. So whether you require fuel to power up, a little boost of energy to keep you going, or a refreshing sip to quench your thirst, the power of plants is here to help you flourish. Discover the vibrant energy of nature's goodness within. Through transforming vegetables, fruits, and botanicals into delicious & dynamic blends, V8 unleashes the power of plants.

### Situation
It was no secret that V8 was known as a "legacy brand." Still, with the rapid rise in juice culture, it was primed to deliver all the benefits younger generations are looking for in an affordable and accessible way. Leveraging the heritage and equity of the brand was vital; it would provide credibility in a constantly shifting category. The trick was to find new ways to tap into the classic and vibrant ingredient combinations to bring a sense of dynamic flourishment that would resonate across the brand's entire product portfolio. Across the brand's product portfolio, a disconnect in visuals, hierarchy, and branding contributed to consumer confusion.

### Solution
In leveraging the existing design equities that give the brand credibility as the original better-for-you drink, it was essential to consider how to evolve the ingredient imagery and brand emotion. Food photography significantly impacts how consumers perceive taste and function; utilizing dynamic cuts and cascading imagery emanating from the logo reinfused the power of plants as a core tenet of the brand. The logo was streamlined to align better and sit within the ever-evolving juice category, where consumers desire simplicity and clarity. With a vast product portfolio and a diverse range of flavors in each segment, the visual identity was crafted to synchronize core elements across the brand.

### Outcome
After the successful launch of the new visual identity system, V8 garnered a lot of earned media coverage. To promote the refreshed look, the brand utilized the developed assets in their "Find It In V8" campaign, which boosted product awareness nationwide. V8 attracted new demographics to its core offerings while retaining its brand loyalists. Innovations such as V8+ Energy saw a significant increase in sales & distribution as younger demographics rallied behind the plant-based energy solution on platforms such as TikTok and Instagram.

## PROJECT 6: Over Easy

- Agency: Hatch SF
- Services: Brand Strategy, Brand & Visual Identity, Brand World Development, Packaging, Illustration, Photography
- Sean's Role: Senior Design Director

### Tagline
"Shining A Light On That Feel-Good Morning Vibe"

### Case Study Structure (unique -- uses Situation/Task/Action format)

### Situation
After a few years on the market as a niche breakfast bar company, Over Easy wanted to broaden its horizons from the direct-to-consumer market and expand into mass retail. They aspired to compete with "Big Breakfast" as a nourishing, delicious, easy option for consumers at any time.

### Task
The primary objective was to evolve the brand's identity to communicate Over Easy's distinct and differentiated nature, setting it up to transition from a niche market to shelves nationwide.

### Action
Over Easy was repositioned as a delightfully natural snack with a healthy spark of confidence. This easy-going, feel-good infectious force was brought to life by grounding the brand in delivering powered-up nutrition, all-day craveability, and being an upliftingly easy choice for consumers.

### Sub-sections with copy:

**"Dawn to Dusk Delights"**
Decadent food photography creates a delightful indulgence that feels oh-so-good that your taste buds will hunger for it all day. From the sensorial honey drip to the soft, warm tones, the oat bars take center stage, elevating the brand's taste appeal.

**"Keepin' It Natural"**
Charming ingredient clusters were illustrated for each flavor. The compositions are placed atop the food photography as if on a pedestal, evoking a sense of reverence and admiration. Each cluster is curated with a playful structure; their organic placement creates a naturally inviting realness.

**"That Feel-Good Ease"**
The simple yet uplifting language was developed to invite consumers to enjoy more than just their nourishingly easy eats. The aim was also to inspire them to be effortlessly themselves and a feel-good infectious force. The tone of voice balances the functional and emotional benefits, leveraged through bold typography with a stylized fluidity.

# SECTION 6: COPY STYLE & TONE ANALYSIS

## Voice Characteristics
Sean's copy consistently demonstrates these patterns:

**Aspirational but grounded.** Every project has an elevated emotional hook ("Unearth Your True Spirit," "Unlock Your Cellular Potential," "A Joyful Force Of Nature") but always ties back to a tangible product truth.

**Sensory language.** Heavy use of physical, tactile descriptors: "melt in your mouth," "oh-so-crunchy," "slow-whipped and ultra creamy," "sweet and smokey nectar," "sensorial honey drip." The copy makes you feel the product.

**Strategic vocabulary blended with warmth.** Terms like "brand blocking," "visual identity system," "design equities," "hierarchy" sit naturally alongside casual phrases like "get your snack on," "let your hair down," "crispy IPA." Never purely corporate, never purely casual.

**Problem/Solution/Results structure.** Nearly every case study follows this arc. The Problem sections are concise and business-focused. The Solution sections are the longest and most descriptive. The Results sections are data-driven with specific metrics.

**Credits are thorough.** Every collaborator is named and linked. Sean positions himself within teams, not above them. Roles range from Designer to Senior Design Director to Creative Director depending on the project.

## Recurring Phrases & Patterns
- "From the ground up" (used in Leisure Project)
- Compound descriptors: "refreshingly lush," "delightfully natural," "artfully stylized"
- Questions as hooks: "What would happen if ice cream loved the planet as much as we do?"
- Bold standalone taglines as section headers
- Mix of first-person ("engaged me as a freelancer") and third-person brand voice

## Tone Spectrum
More playful/energetic: Gimme, Over Easy, Picture Day
More sophisticated/strategic: V8, Elenita, Mimio
More narrative/storytelling: Leisure Project, Alec's Ice Cream
More straightforward/professional: Gig Car Share, Licensing Artwork, Child Aid

## What the Copy Does NOT Do
- No jargon without context
- No self-aggrandizing (positions work within team context)
- No generic marketing-speak ("synergy," "leverage," "optimize")
- No long-winded intros -- gets to the brand story fast
- Does not oversell results -- lets specific metrics speak
